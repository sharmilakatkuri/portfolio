My Projects

Sentimental analysis on amazon products
This project focuses on analyzing Amazon product reviews using machine learning and natural language processing to understand customer sentiment. The goal is to classify reviews as positive, negative, or neutral. It involves data preprocessing, feature extraction, and the use of models like SVM and neural networks. The insights gained help businesses improve customer satisfaction, enhance product quality, and drive revenue growth by making informed decisions based on user feedback.
Funnel Analysis at GarbhaGudi IVF Center
In this project, I conducted funnel analysis to track and optimize the patient journey at GarbhaGudi IVF Center. Using clinical data, I identified stages where patient drop-offs occurred and calculated conversion rates. Key performance indicators such as consultation-to-treatment and treatment-to-successful outcome ratios were analyzed. The findings helped suggest data-driven improvements for patient retention and operational efficiency.
Library Attendance Analysis
This project focused on analyzing and forecasting student visitation patterns to the Anurag University Library. I studied gender-wise attendance trends, peak usage times, and popular book genres across locations. Forecasting techniques were applied to predict attendance for the next two years. Additional analysis identified the most borrowed titles, authors, and departments. The insights support better resource allocation and space management.
Heart Disease Prediction using Bio-Inspired Algorithms
Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide, highlighting the urgent need for timely and accurate diagnosis. This study aims to develop an efficient predictive system using advanced optimization techniques. The proposed model integrates Genetic Algorithm with BAT and BEE algorithms to enhance prediction accuracy. Comparative analysis demonstrates that the hybrid approach outperforms individual algorithms, offering a reliable solution for early heart disease detection.
